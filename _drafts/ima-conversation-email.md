## Email draft

**Subject:** Quick heads-up on the new Fabrick carbon calculator + an offer

---

Hi [name],

We have just put a new version of our open carbon calculator live at fabrick.agency/materials. I want to flag it to you directly before any IMA member sees it cold, and to put a small idea on the table.

What it does. A specifier puts in a build-up, the tool ranks the materials on embodied carbon (ICE Database) and surfaces the trade-offs alongside: thermal performance per mm, fire rating, indicative cost. It is comparison, not a verdict. If a specifier has a wall depth constraint, the tool now makes PIR's headline λ value (~0.022 W/m·K) visible against alternatives at 0.038-0.040 — which is the conversation the industry should already be having and often is not.

Why I wanted you to see it first. The carbon ranking on its own makes PIR look like a "high carbon" choice. That is true on the kgCO₂e/kg axis. It is not the whole specification picture, and our methodology page makes that point explicitly. The new multi-criteria view is designed precisely so a specifier sees PIR's thermal and depth advantage at the same time as the carbon number. We think this is the honest framing and a better one for your members than what most calculators do.

The offer. Most of our values for PIR (and every other material) are industry-average from the ICE Database. Where IMA members hold product-specific EPDs that beat the database average — and several do, materially — the calculator currently cannot see it. We would like to set up a route to ingest verified, product-level EPDs from IMA members and credit them in the calculator and methodology page. A specifier choosing the Kingspan / Recticel / Celotex / Xtratherm spec they are actually buying gets a sharper carbon number; the manufacturer's EPD investment gets visibility in front of a specifier audience.

Would you be up for a 30-minute call in the next fortnight? Happy to walk you through the tool, the data behind it, and what an IMA-member EPD pipeline could look like. I would rather get this right with you than have it become a thing we are reacting to.

Best,
Tom

Tom Colgan
Director of Digital Services, Fabrick
